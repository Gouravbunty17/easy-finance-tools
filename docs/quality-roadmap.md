# EasyFinanceTools Quality Roadmap

Last updated: May 7, 2026

This roadmap exists to keep EasyFinanceTools from drifting back into thin utility content, affiliate-first pages, or generic finance explanations. The site should feel like a Canadian decision-support brand: transparent, source-linked, practical, and clear about its limits.

## Pages Improved in Phase 3

- Added official source infrastructure for CRA, Government of Canada, FCAC, CMHC, and Bank of Canada references.
- Added visible official-source blocks to TFSA, RRSP, FHSA, mortgage, mortgage affordability, income tax, capital gains, CPP/OAS, dividend, account-decision, and shared education-article pages.
- Added topic hubs for TFSA, RRSP, FHSA, dividend investing, mortgages, and retirement.
- Improved homepage positioning around source-linked decision support, topic paths, and methodology.
- Strengthened About, Methodology, and Editorial Standards with clearer maintenance, review, and authority-language boundaries.

## YMYL Quality Checklist

- The page has one clear H1 and a unique title and meta description.
- The page explains what the tool or article does and does not do.
- Important assumptions, formulas, or data limits are visible.
- Official Canadian sources are linked where rules, tax, benefits, or mortgages are discussed.
- The author/founder information is honest and does not invent credentials.
- The page avoids guaranteed outcomes, exaggerated claims, fake social proof, and pressure language.
- Affiliate or referral links appear after useful educational context and include a clear disclosure.
- The page links to a next calculator, guide, or hub so users are not stranded.
- The mobile layout is readable and result sections are not cluttered by ads or unrelated CTAs.
- If the page cannot meet these standards, it should be improved, noindexed, or removed from core journeys.
